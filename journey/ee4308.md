# EE4308 journey — Autonomous Robot Systems

Making a TurtleBot drive itself: A* planning + a regulated pure-pursuit
controller, built and tuned over two weeks of commits on
[my project branch](https://github.com/Javachipsfrappe/ee4308_project1/tree/2520-proj1).

---

## Iteration 1 — controller first, no planner (Feb 27)

`Pure pursuit implementation without planner`

I started with the controller alone, feeding it hand-made paths. **Why this
order:** if the controller can't follow a known-good path, debugging it through
a planner just multiplies the suspects. Classic pure pursuit: find the closest
point on the path, walk forward to a lookahead point, steer toward it.

## Iteration 2 — the robot corners too fast (Mar 2)

`Add curvature-based velocity regulation and tuning updates`

Constant linear velocity meant the robot overshot every tight turn — the
lookahead geometry is only valid if you're slow enough to actually track it.
Fix: **regulate linear velocity by path curvature**, slowing down when the
path bends (the "regulated" in Regulated Pure Pursuit).

## Iteration 3 — planning, and a pivot (Mar 2, same night)

`Implement proximity-based controller (no curvature optimization)`

The big commit: **A\* global planner** (open-list search over the occupancy
grid, ~320 lines of planner.cpp) plus new test maps, and a controller pivot —
regulating velocity by **proximity to obstacles** rather than curvature alone.
**Why the pivot:** curvature regulation slows you in bends but happily drives
fast past a wall on a straight path; distance-to-obstacle is the signal that
actually correlates with "about to hit something."

## Iteration 4 — putting it together and killing the residual error (Mar 6)

`Enhance Controller with new parameters and heuristics` ·
`Enhance Controller with Pure Pursuit and PI controller` · `Update proj1.yaml`

Final form, all parameterized through ROS 2 params for tuning without recompiles:

- **Regulated Pure Pursuit** with an **adaptive lookahead** (scaled by speed via
  `lookahead_gain` — short lookahead cuts corners at speed, long lookahead is
  sluggish near the goal, so it shouldn't be constant)
- **Both heuristics active**: curvature threshold + proximity threshold on
  linear velocity
- **PI control on heading** (`angular_kp`, `angular_ki`): pure proportional
  steering left a steady-state heading offset on long straight segments; the
  integral term removes it (with the usual care about windup)

Then a tuning pass in `proj1.yaml` — where most of the actual hours went.

## What this taught me

- Build the pipeline in testable layers: controller on known paths → planner →
  integration. Every commit above stayed runnable.
- "Working algorithm" and "tuned algorithm" are different milestones, weeks
  apart. The literature gives you pure pursuit; it doesn't give you your robot's
  lookahead gain.
- The same lesson as [RoboMaster](robomaster.md): the fix is usually a better
  *signal* (proximity vs curvature), not a fancier controller.
